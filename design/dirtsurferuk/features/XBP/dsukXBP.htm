150602: XBP (xtreme boarding @ priory) is a brand new course which opened at the beginning of June. Overlooked by a huge manor house, it runs between the orchards of the huge priory farm estate. We were joined on our ride by Bryn - a friendly four-wheeler, and new girl Becky, who we eventually persuaded to have a go on Bryn's board. The whole place has a really friendly attitude and is a really good example of mountain boarding at it's best - no-one cares what board you're riding or how good you are. This was quite encouraging as we stacked so many times on the kicker ramp! After about five attempts we had worked our the basic technique and ripped the grass up with our (new!) pads and the dirtsurfer's headset. Meanwhile Bryn was shooting down (and up) the huge freestyle jump and pulling all sorts of crazy moves.

At the top of the slope is the start ramp for the half pipe and boarder cross course. The half pipe run is very fast and is made especially hard for dirtsurfers since it runs directly into the speed bumps, which as their name suggests need to be ridden quite slowly (or your nose plants into the face of the next hump). This was especially a problem for Tim, who was riding without a brake!

On the boarder cross course more attention was required to choose a good line as there were some ground clearance issues, but after a few practice runs we all managed to make it down in one go(!), and by the end Bryn had it totally nailed.

The learner and slalom runs also provided a lot of entertainment and opportunity for racing. They also allowed us to demonstrate how fast the dirtsurfer really can be, crusing past Bryn and carrying us round the bottom of the hill and starting back up the other side.

bryn The big freestyle jump was somewhat more daunting. We started off by trying to roll in from the side - to give us a bit more control over the speed, which was ok when it worked, but approaching the lip at an angle led to quite a few crashes. Ben tried rolling in from the top of the start ramp (with his brake fully on!) and still managed to get a bit air off the lip.

After much persuasion we finally got Becky off the bench and onto a board. Using elbow pads on her knees, she sped down the hill and only stacked once!

XBP is a really good center, with something for people of all abilities, from the nursery slopes to the half pipe. The other great thing is that there is still a lot of potential for improvements too, so the center should get better every year. The lift to the top is also another huge bonus as well as the great atmosphere.
