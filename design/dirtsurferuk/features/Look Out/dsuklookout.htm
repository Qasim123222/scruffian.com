a trip in to the depths of bracknell forest. 

 After warming up a bit on some of the wide gravel paths we soon found some twisty singletrack and got stuck in. It was surprisingly easy to hold your course along the thin paths and even to get smoothly round some of the sharper corners. It wasn't long before we came across an obstacle  -  a fat log blocking the path. I tried to jump this a few times, and although me and the dirtsurfer made it over, we did not to do together, as the picture shows! We wound our way through the forest, picking up speed on some of the steeper paths. I tried jumping a smaller log, which was much easier! Then we noticed a small opening in the undergrowth above us. A little exploration revealed some seriously twisty track which was so overgrown you had to crouch to make it through some sections. Perhaps surprisingly this track was made especially difficult as the path was not on much of a slope. Since the board gains stability with speed, the slower run down this slope made the board unstable and the track harder to ride. After landing in various shrubs, we were sent us speeding back to the main path again from which we noticed some steep chutes made by mountain bikes. These really got the adrenaline pumping (even if the irate walker complaining us being in the way of the trees didn't!). It was difficult to kick off from the top of the chute, since the start was almost vertical so the board touched down, but as soon as you got rolling the speed picked up very quickly and would have been enough to attempt the jump built at the bottom (with more guts and protection!) I decided not to use the bikers run out but instead roll into the undergrowth to kill off the speed which worked pretty well, although I was probably lucky not to get a puncture!

twisty twisty - watch the tree
  From the top of these chutes we saw what we had been hoping for. Unfortunately the sun makes the pictures quite bad, but way above our heads loomed a long steep slope. The surface was very uneven, the gradient steep and the run out long. Perfect. I admit that I didn't have the guts to try it from the top, but even starting from about 10 meters down the run was sketchy. This goes to show that the limits of this board are easily stretched but also that there are a lot of challenges left to attempt.

 

The look out has plenty to keep you amused for a long time - the forest is very big. Unfortunately it takes some looking to find the best parts of the wood. However, there is a huge range of riding for almost all skill levels, from wide shallow paths, to crazily steep slopes with no path, or impossibly twisty singletrack! It is unsuitable for beginners though as there are not long grassy slopes, and falling off on gravel is very painful. Don't travel especially, but if you are in the area definitely check this out.
