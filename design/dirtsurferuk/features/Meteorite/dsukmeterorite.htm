meteorite hill The North Downs runs like a spine straight through Kent, covered with fields and a smattering of sheep. As we drove along the feet of this stretch of the downs (westwell), we scanned the hills looking for some good dirtsurfing. Up to our left we spotted a suitably steep field, characterized by what looked like a large meteorite (hence the name). On the left of the field there was a steep drop with no run out and a barbed wire fence at the bottom. Taking the run at an angle made it a bit less steep and gave us a longer ride as well as allowing us to run out along the bottom of the hill. We then started carving across the huge field, getting across it three times without even reaching the bottom. On the other side of the field we found a weird ditch thing which sloped round creating the perfect natural berm. We had just got this sorted as we heard cries of "oi, what do you think you're doing?". Thinking better of answering this question we made out way back slowly across the field to the road, followed by the farmer, who seemed to be intent on scaring the sheep as much as possible with his land rover and dogs. On the way back up to the car we spotted a small wood on the right, leading up to the top of the field. The path was fairly straight, but had some sharp corners, and ditches either side. We hammered down between the foliage either side, ducking overhanging branches and leaning right into the bends, before losing control and ploughing into the nettles and brambles or nose-diving into the ditches either side of the path. Then we climbed a tree (you don't need a reason). We went home via our local forest where we burnt around on the main path a bit before having a go at building a jump. This was not all that successful as the landing ramp was just bumpy scrub land covered with weeds and running straight into a bush.   
 
 
 
   
 
We did manage to get a rough idea of a good shape - more will follow when we have this sorted out - or does anyone have any ideas? The North Downs has lots of great fields ideal for boarding. You have to be careful to check for rocks before you begin the run though, as some slopes are covered with large ones which have been known to buckle wheels! However the good slopes seem to be owned by unfriendly farmers who don't realize the surfing potential! (Have a look here!)
