board tips
in the UK boards don't come with the standard leg v-brake. you can order this from dirtsurfer, or you can make your own handbrake, whether you have a leg brake or not. you can buy v-brake arms, cable, cable housing and levers from a bike shop (or take them off an old bike). ask for short arm v-brakes as these won't interfere with the dead man's brake. then saw the end off an old handlebar and attach the lever (and grip). if you are fitting the hand brake with a leg brake you need to fix the cable to the cable of the leg brake - you can use a cable splitter from a BMX to do this
 
although scratches on your board are inevitable, you can reduce the wear on it by making some simple protection for the sides. cut open an inner tube open and wrap this round the parts of the board that touch the ground.  cover with a few reels of insulating tape.
 
if you have noticed that your board keeps touching down more than it used to, then you have probably bent it. find out how to unbend it!
