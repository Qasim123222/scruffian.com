how to skate similar advice is given on the official dirtsurfer site, but this gives you another angle which might help
to begin, put your rear foot on the board at about 45 degrees to it, and balance it upright with this foot. does anyone have any good skating picturess?!
when you can hold the board in this position, push yourself and the board forward with your front foot. make sure you push fairly hard, so that the board is going fast enough to be stable.
to help you in keeping upright try and keep your rear leg bent, but held firmly in position. as always with things like this, you go where you look,  so concentrate on where you want to go, not on the front wheel or anything else.
dirtsurfer advise doing 2 long pushes before putting your front foot on, to give the board enough stability. this is good advise but it's not set in stone. if something else works better for you then do that instead.
again, advise which is often true is relax, enjoy yourself and don't get frustrated.
when you have mastered this and your balance on the board starts improving, it becomes less important to keep putting your front foot back on. then you can really start pumping up the speed by really kicking with your front foot.
 
other tips for riding on the road:
look at the pictures of guy setting the land speed record on the dirsurfer. he is crouching down over his rear foot. this makes the board go much faster by cutting out all of the wind resistance. this may sound ridiculous, but if you try it along side a friend on a dirtsufer or a even a bike (as long as they don't pedal), you notice how much quicker you go. you can achieve this by just crouching down, but if you work your rear foot out of its binding, then this allows you to crouch over it and get much lower. try it - you'll be surprised
